📘 Complete Tenses Grammar Guide

📌 What Are Tenses?

Tenses in English grammar express the **time** at which an action takes place. There are three main tenses — Present, Past, and Future — and each has four forms.

🕰️ Present Tense

1. Simple Present

Structure: Subject + base verb (s/es for he/she/it)

Examples: She walks to school. / They play football.

Uses: Daily routines, facts, habits.

  • Use -s/es with he/she/it
  • Don’t add -s with I/You/We/They

2. Present Continuous

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing

Examples: I am studying now.

Uses: Actions happening now or around the present moment.

3. Present Perfect

Structure: Subject + has/have + past participle

Examples: She has finished her work.

Uses: Completed actions with connection to the present.

4. Present Perfect Continuous

Structure: Subject + has/have been + verb+ing

Examples: They have been studying for two hours.

Uses: Actions that started in the past and are still continuing.
