Tenses in English grammar express the **time** at which an action takes place. There are three main tenses ‚Äî Present, Past, and Future ‚Äî and each has four forms.

üï∞Ô∏è Present Tense

1. Simple Present

Structure: Subject + base verb (s/es for he/she/it)

Examples: She walks to school. / They play football.

Uses: Daily routines, facts, habits.

  • Use -s/es with he/she/it
  • Don‚Äôt add -s with I/You/We/They

2. Present Continuous

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing

Examples: I am studying now.

Uses: Actions happening now or around the present moment.

3. Present Perfect

Structure: Subject + has/have + past participle

Examples: She has finished her work.

Uses: Completed actions with connection to the present.

4. Present Perfect Continuous

Structure: Subject + has/have been + verb+ing

Examples: They have been studying for two hours.

Uses: Actions that started in the past and are still continuing.

üï∞Ô∏è Past Tense

1. Simple Past

Definition: Describes a completed action in the past.

Structure: Subject + V2 (past verb) + object

Examples: She wrote a poem. / They watched a movie.

Usage: Used for actions completed at a definite time in the past.

Common Mistakes:

  • ‚ùå She write a letter yesterday. ‚úÖ She wrote a letter yesterday.

2. Past Continuous

Definition: Describes an action that was ongoing in the past.

Structure: Subject + was/were + V1+ing + object

Examples: I was reading when she called. / They were playing.

3. Past Perfect

Definition: Expresses an action completed before another past action.

Structure: Subject + had + V3 + object

Examples: He had left before I arrived.

4. Past Perfect Continuous

Definition: Shows that an action was ongoing in the past before another past action/time.

Structure: Subject + had been + V1+ing + object

Examples: She had been waiting for two hours before the bus came.

üï∞Ô∏è Future Tense

1. Simple Future

Definition: Describes actions that will happen in the future.

Structure: Subject + will/shall + V1 + object

Examples: I will call you tomorrow. / She will travel next week.

2. Future Continuous

Definition: Action that will be in progress at a future time.

Structure: Subject + will be + V1+ing + object

Examples: This time tomorrow, I will be flying to Delhi.

3. Future Perfect

Definition: Describes a future action that will be completed before a specified time.

Structure: Subject + will have + V3 + object

Examples: She will have finished the book by tomorrow.

4. Future Perfect Continuous

Definition: Focuses on the duration of an activity that will be in progress before a time in the future.

Structure: Subject + will have been + V1+ing + object

Examples: By 2025, I will have been teaching for 10 years.
