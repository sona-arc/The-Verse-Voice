üï∞Ô∏è Present Tense

The Present Tense describes actions happening now or regularly.

  • Simple Present: I eat lunch every day.
  • Present Continuous: She is studying now.
  • Present Perfect: They have finished the work.
  • Present Perfect Continuous: We have been working since morning.
Form Structure Example
Simple Present Subject + base verb (+s/es) He walks to school.
Present Continuous Subject + is/am/are + verb+ing I am reading a book.
Present Perfect Subject + has/have + past participle They have completed the task.
Present Perfect Continuous Subject + has/have been + verb+ing She has been dancing for hours.

üï∞Ô∏è Past Tense

The Past Tense describes actions that already happened.

  • Simple Past: I walked to school.
  • Past Continuous: They were playing football.
  • Past Perfect: She had left before we arrived.
  • Past Perfect Continuous: He had been working all day.
Form Structure Example
Simple Past Subject + past verb She sang beautifully.
Past Continuous Subject + was/were + verb+ing They were running fast.
Past Perfect Subject + had + past participle He had eaten already.
Past Perfect Continuous Subject + had been + verb+ing I had been waiting for 2 hours.

üï∞Ô∏è Future Tense

The Future Tense describes actions that will happen later.

  • Simple Future: I will go to college.
  • Future Continuous: She will be studying tomorrow.
  • Future Perfect: They will have completed the project.
  • Future Perfect Continuous: He will have been driving for hours.
Form Structure Example
Simple Future Subject + will + base verb I will visit my grandma.
Future Continuous Subject + will be + verb+ing She will be sleeping.
Future Perfect Subject + will have + past participle They will have left by 9 PM.
Future Perfect Continuous Subject + will have been + verb+ing He will have been working for 10 hours.
