1. Simple Past

Definition: Used for actions completed in the past at a specific time.

Examples: She danced yesterday. / They went to the market.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + V2 + Object
NegativeSubject + did not + V1 + Object
InterrogativeDid + subject + V1 + Object?

2. Past Continuous

Definition: Describes actions that were happening at a particular time in the past.

Examples: I was reading. / They were eating dinner.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + was/were + V1-ing
NegativeSubject + was/were not + V1-ing
InterrogativeWas/Were + subject + V1-ing?

3. Past Perfect

Definition: Refers to an action completed before another action in the past.

Examples: She had left before I arrived.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + had + V3
NegativeSubject + had not + V3
InterrogativeHad + subject + V3?

4. Past Perfect Continuous

Definition: Emphasizes duration of an action up to a point in the past.

Examples: They had been waiting for two hours.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + had been + V1-ing
NegativeSubject + had not been + V1-ing
InterrogativeHad + subject + been + V1-ing?

üï∞Ô∏è Future Tense

1. Simple Future

Definition: Refers to an action that will happen later than now.

Examples: She will come tomorrow. / I will call you.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + will + V1
NegativeSubject + will not + V1
InterrogativeWill + subject + V1?

2. Future Continuous

Definition: Describes an action that will be happening at a certain future time.

Examples: I will be studying at 8 PM. / They will be playing.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + will be + V1-ing
NegativeSubject + will not be + V1-ing
InterrogativeWill + subject + be + V1-ing?

3. Future Perfect

Definition: Refers to a completed action before a certain future time.

Examples: I will have finished the work by 6 PM.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + will have + V3
NegativeSubject + will not have + V3
InterrogativeWill + subject + have + V3?

4. Future Perfect Continuous

Definition: Describes the duration of an action up to a point in the future.

Examples: She will have been working for 5 years by next month.

TypeStructure
AffirmativeSubject + will have been + V1-ing
NegativeSubject + will not have been + V1-ing
InterrogativeWill + subject + have been + V1-ing?
