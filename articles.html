Articles

Articles are words that define a noun as specific or unspecific. In English, there are two types of articles: definite (the) and indefinite (a, an).

Definite Article: "The"

Used before a specific noun that is known to the reader/listener. Example: The book on the table is mine.

Indefinite Articles: "A" and "An"

Used before non-specific nouns. "A" is used before words starting with a consonant sound; "An" is used before words starting with a vowel sound. Example: I saw a cat. / She is an artist.
